What did the skeletal survey reveal about the child's injuries?

The skeletal survey revealed the following about the child's injuries:

Left Humerus:

Type of Fracture: Spiral fracture of the humeral diaphysis
Estimated Age of Fracture: 24-48 hours
Mechanism of Injury: Torsional/rotational mechanism ​1​
Right Femur:

Type of Fracture: Transverse fracture of the femoral shaft
Estimated Age of Fracture: 3-5 days ​1​
Additional Findings:

Fractures are at different stages of healing, indicating two separate injury events occurring at different times.
No additional acute or healing fractures identified on the skeletal survey.
No metabolic bone disease pattern (e.g., osteopenia, Wormian bones) to suggest an underlying bone fragility disorder ​1​.
Medical Opinion:

The fracture pattern is inconsistent with a fall from a standard crib height.
The spiral fracture of the left humerus indicates torsional force.
Radiographic findings suggest the femur fracture is approximately 24-48 hours older than the humerus fracture, indicating two separate injury events.
A single unwitnessed fall from a crib in a 3-year-old child would not be expected to produce bilateral long bone fractures affecting both an upper and lower extremity.
The different stages of healing observed on radiographic imaging confirm that these injuries did not occur during a single event.
The spiral morphology of the humeral fracture is a recognized indicator of applied rotational force, which is not consistent with a simple fall mechanism ​1​.

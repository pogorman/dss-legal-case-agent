## 1. Summary

This analysis traces the complete chain of ownership for the property at 4763 Griscom Street, Philadelphia PA 19124, from its earliest recorded transfer in 1999 through the current owner, GEENA LLC. The property has changed hands six times over 25 years, passing through two sheriff sales, a period of rapid flipping, and a city-ordered demolition. The assessed value has collapsed from a peak of $146,000 (the 2004 purchase price) to a current assessed value of $24,800 — an 83% decline.

During his ownership, Michael L. Bryan took out a mortgage with CIT Group/Consumer Finance Inc. on January 10, 2002 (Document ID 50387128). This mortgage was subsequently assigned from CIT Group to Altegra Credit Company on August 28, 2002 (Document ID 50518147), and then from Altegra to Alaska Seaboard Partners Limited Partnership and Mortgage Electronic Registration Systems (MERS) on August 11, 2003 (Document ID 50732045). The rapid assignment of this single mortgage through three financial entities is a pattern commonly associated with subprime lending during the pre-2008 housing bubble.

One mortgage included MERS as a co-grantee. These simultaneous purchase-and-mortgage transactions suggest an acquisition financed entirely through debt, potentially at terms favorable to the seller or broker rather than the buyer.

## 4. Key Findings

1. **Two foreclosures in six years.** The property was sold at sheriff sale in 2013 (Habeeb to DeJesus) and again in 2019 (Martinez to GEENA LLC). Each foreclosure resulted in a catastrophic price decline.

2. **Predatory pricing in 2004.** Bryan sold the property to Habeeb for $146,000 when the fair market value was approximately $53,155. Habeeb immediately took out two mortgages, suggesting the purchase was debt-financed at an inflated price. This is consistent with predatory lending patterns common in the mid-2000s.

3. **Rapid flipping in 2014.** DeJesus → Luong → Martinez within 26 days, with minimal price increases ($20K → $21.5K). This pattern suggests coordinated or speculative trading rather than genuine investment.

4. **City bore the demolition cost.** The building was demolished at city expense under the L&I Contractual Services program. Neither the owner at the time (Martinez) nor the subsequent purchaser (GEENA LLC) bore any of the demolition costs.

5. **Post-acquisition neglect.** Since GEENA LLC acquired the property in December 2019, it has accumulated 43 additional violation investigations with no development activity, no license applications, and no permits filed. The lot has been held as bare land for over six years.

6. **Net value destruction.** Over 25 years, the property's value trajectory tells the story of a neighborhood asset — a single-family home — that was inflated, foreclosed, flipped, demolished, and abandoned. The current assessed value of $24,800 is less than the original 1999 purchase price of $10,650 in inflation-adjusted terms.

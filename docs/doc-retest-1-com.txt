What evidence contradicts the parents' claim that Jaylen fell from his crib?

Evidence Contradicting the Parents' Claim That Jaylen Fell from His Crib

1. Medical Evidence

The fracture pattern is inconsistent with a fall from a standard crib height. Jaylen sustained a transverse fracture of the right femur (aged 3-5 days) and a spiral fracture of the left humerus (aged 24-48 hours). The spiral fracture indicates a torsional force, not a simple fall. Radiographic findings show the femur fracture is older than the humerus fracture, confirming two separate injury events days apart. A single unwitnessed fall from a crib would not produce bilateral long bone fractures in both an upper and lower extremity, nor would it result in injuries at different stages of healing​1​.
2. Physician Assessment

Dr. Anita Chowdhury, the attending pediatrician, stated: "In my medical opinion, these injuries are non-accidental. A three-year-old falling from a crib would not sustain bilateral long bone fractures of this nature. The different healing stages confirm at least two separate trauma events occurring days apart"​1​.
3. DSS and Law Enforcement Findings

Both parents initially claimed Jaylen fell from his crib, but their accounts were inconsistent with each other and with the medical evidence. Ms. Holloway later revised her account, stating she heard a loud thump at 9:30 PM and that Mr. Webb was in or near the room at that time. She also disclosed that she had seen Mr. Webb grab Jaylen roughly by the arm on prior occasions. Mr. Webb denied being in the room after putting Jaylen to bed and denied any rough handling. These inconsistencies, along with the medical evidence, led DSS to recommend emergency removal and further investigation​2​​​3​.
4. Nursing and Law Enforcement Observations

ER nursing staff noted that the pattern of bruising on Jaylen's upper arm was not consistent with a fall from a crib and was more consistent with being grabbed or restrained with force​4​.
5. Delay in Seeking Medical Care

There was a significant delay between the suspected injury event and the parents seeking medical attention, which medical staff found concerning​3​.
Summary of Contradictory Evidence

Injuries at different stages of healing, indicating multiple events.
Fracture types (spiral and transverse) inconsistent with a simple fall.
Medical opinion of non-accidental trauma.
Inconsistent and evolving parental accounts.
Bruising patterns suggestive of forceful restraint.
Delay in seeking care for significant injuries.
These points collectively contradict the parents' claim that Jaylen's injuries resulted from a fall from his crib​1​​​2​​​4​​​3​.

What were the findings of Jaylen Webb's skeletal survey

Findings of Jaylen Webb's Skeletal Survey

Radiology and Clinical Findings

The skeletal survey identified two fractures:
Right femur: Transverse mid-shaft fracture, estimated age 3-5 days, with periosteal reaction and early callus formation. This fracture predates the reported incident and is older than the humerus fracture.
Left humerus: Spiral (oblique) fracture of the humeral diaphysis, estimated age 24-48 hours. The spiral pattern is consistent with a torsional/rotational mechanism of injury.
Both fractures are at different stages of healing, indicating two separate injury events occurring at different times.
The described mechanism of injury (single fall from a crib) is biomechanically inconsistent with bilateral long bone fractures at different healing stages in a child of this age.
No additional acute or healing fractures were identified on the remainder of the skeletal survey. No evidence of metabolic bone disease or underlying bone fragility disorder was found​1​.
Physician Assessment

The fracture pattern is inconsistent with a fall from a standard crib height.
The spiral fracture of the left humerus indicates torsional force.
Radiographic findings suggest the femur fracture is approximately 24-48 hours older than the humerus fracture, confirming two separate injury events.
The injuries are non-accidental in origin and are the result of at least two separate inflicted trauma events occurring over a period of days​1​.
Summary of Skeletal Survey

No fractures were detected in areas outside the right femur and left humerus.
No rib, skull, spine, pelvis, or other extremity fractures were found.
No metaphyseal corner fractures (bucket-handle fractures) or other osseous abnormalities were present​1​.
Conclusion

The skeletal survey confirmed two separate fractures at different healing stages, both inconsistent with accidental trauma, and no other fractures or bone disease​1​.

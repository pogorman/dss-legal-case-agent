Below are the cases and key details involving Termination of Parental Rights (TPR):

1. DSS v. Price (Case No. 2024-DR-15-0341, Richland County, SC)

Children Involved: Amari Price (DOB: 9/28/2017) and Destiny Price (DOB: 5/14/2019)
Grounds for TPR:
Substantiated child neglect: Children were found in unsanitary conditions, with no food, no running water, and drug paraphernalia accessible in the home. The mother, Crystal Price, appeared impaired during the DSS visit.
Failure to remedy: Despite a court-ordered treatment plan (substance abuse evaluation/treatment, parenting classes, stable housing, drug screens), the mother failed to make substantial progress over more than six months.
Substance abuse: Diagnosed Opioid Use Disorder, moderate severity, with ongoing positive drug screens and missed appointments.
Father’s Status: Brandon Price voluntarily relinquished his parental rights on November 20, 2023.
Current Status: The children remain in foster care with the Patterson family, who wish to adopt them. The Guardian ad Litem and DSS recommend TPR and adoption as in the children’s best interest.
Key Orders and Filings:
Emergency Removal Order (August 8, 2023)
Order Following Probable Cause Hearing (August 15, 2023)
Order Following Ninety-Day Review Hearing (November 14, 2023)
Order Accepting Voluntary Relinquishment of Parental Rights (November 20, 2023)
Petition for Termination of Parental Rights (February 8, 2024)
Order Following TPR Probable Cause Hearing (April 22, 2024)
Merits hearing scheduled for July 15, 2024​1​​​2​​​3​.
2. DSS v. Webb & Holloway (Case No. 2024-DR-42-0892, Spartanburg County, SC)

Child Involved: Jaylen Webb (DOB: 4/10/2021)
Grounds for Emergency Removal (potential TPR):
Non-accidental trauma: The child was admitted to the hospital with a bilateral femur fracture and a spiral fracture of the left humerus, with injuries inconsistent with the parents’ explanation (fall from crib) and at different stages of healing.
Medical and DSS assessment determined imminent risk of harm and recommended removal.
Current Status: Child placed in kinship foster care with maternal grandmother, pending further investigation and court review. TPR proceedings may be considered depending on the outcome of the investigation and subsequent hearings​4​.

Substantiated neglect or abuse

Summary of TPR Case Elements

TPR cases typically involve substantiated neglect, failure to comply with court-ordered treatment plans, substance abuse, and inability to provide minimally adequate care.
Proceedings include emergency removal, probable cause hearings, review hearings, voluntary relinquishment, and formal TPR petitions.
Statutory grounds are cited in court filings and orders, and the best interest of the child is a central consideration​2​.

Case No. 2024-DR-15-0341 DSS v. Price
This case was heard in the Fifth Judicial Circuit Family Court, Richland County, South Carolina.
The South Carolina Department of Social Services (DSS) filed for termination of parental rights regarding two minor children, Amari Price and Destiny Price.
Grounds for termination included:
Child neglect: The children were found living in unsanitary conditions, with no food, no running water, and drug paraphernalia present. The sole custodial parent appeared impaired.
Failure to remedy: The respondent failed to remedy the conditions that led to removal for more than six months, despite a court-ordered treatment plan and reasonable efforts by DSS.
Substance abuse: The respondent was diagnosed with Opioid Use Disorder and demonstrated inconsistent compliance with treatment, including positive drug screens and missed appointments.
The biological father, Brandon Price, voluntarily relinquished his parental rights, which was accepted by the court.
The children have been placed in foster care and are thriving in their current placement. The foster family has expressed intent to adopt if termination is granted.
The court found probable cause to proceed with a merits hearing on the petition for termination of parental rights​1​.
